---
description: Conducts a comprehensive senior frontend engineer performance review and determines eligibility for a 1.0 rating.
---

## Step 1: Review Context Initialization
- Assume the role of a Senior Engineering Reviewer.
- Confirm scope: frontend architecture, UI platforms, design systems, and developer tooling.
- Acknowledge breadth of experience across modern and legacy frontend frameworks and ecosystems.

## Step 2: Frontend Architecture & System Design
// turbo
- Evaluate frontend architecture, layering, and separation of concerns.
- Review state management, routing, data-fetching, and rendering strategies.
- Assess long-term scalability and maintainability of the frontend codebase.
- Assign a rating between 0.0 and 1.0 with evidence-based justification.

## Step 3: Framework & Tech Stack Mastery
// turbo
- Evaluate depth and breadth of experience across major frontend frameworks and tooling.
- Review ability to select appropriate frameworks and libraries based on context.
- Identify examples of successful migrations, upgrades, or deprecations.
- Assign a rating between 0.0 and 1.0 with justification.

## Step 4: Performance Optimization & Web Fundamentals
- Assess performance tuning across rendering, network, and asset delivery.
- Review use of profiling tools, code-splitting, caching, and build optimizations.
- Identify measurable improvements to load time, responsiveness, or runtime performance.
- Assign a rating between 0.0 and 1.0 with justification.

## Step 5: UX Quality, Accessibility & Standards
// turbo
- Evaluate adherence to UX best practices and accessibility standards.
- Review implementation of design systems and consistency across interfaces.
- Identify improvements to usability, inclusivity, and user satisfaction.
- Assign a rating between 0.0 and 1.0 with justification.

## Step 6: Code Quality, Testing & Tooling
- Assess code readability, reusability, and testing discipline.
- Review unit, integration, and end-to-end test coverage.
- Evaluate contributions to frontend tooling, CI, or developer experience.
- Assign a rating between 0.0 and 1.0 with justification.

## Step 7: Cross-Functional Collaboration
- Evaluate collaboration with design, product, backend, and QA teams.
- Review ability to translate product requirements into technical solutions.
- Identify examples of proactive alignment and dependency management.
- Assign a rating between 0.0 and 1.0 with justification.

## Step 8: Leadership, Mentorship & Influence
// turbo
- Assess mentorship of junior and mid-level frontend engineers.
- Review leadership in setting frontend standards and best practices.
- Identify examples of raising frontend quality across teams.
- Assign a rating between 0.0 and 1.0 with justification.

## Step 9: Business Impact & Product Outcomes
- Identify measurable business outcomes tied to frontend work.
- Review impact on user engagement, conversion, performance metrics, or delivery speed.
- Assess alignment between frontend decisions and product strategy.
- Assign a rating between 0.0 and 1.0 with justification.

## Step 10: Scoring Summary
- Present all category ratings in a structured table.
- Validate consistency between evidence and assigned scores.
- Highlight any frontend-specific risks or dependencies.

## Step 11: Overall Performance Verdict
- Deliver a clear final performance rating.
- Explicitly state whether performance meets the criteria for a 1.0 score.
- Justify the verdict based on cumulative technical and business impact.

## Step 12: Forward-Looking Development Guidance
- Identify growth opportunities appropriate for a high-impact senior frontend engineer.
- Focus on increasing architectural scope, platform ownership, or organizational influence.
- Clearly distinguish development opportunities from performance gaps.
